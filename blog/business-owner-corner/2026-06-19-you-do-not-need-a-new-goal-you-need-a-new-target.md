It is June. And if you made a goal in January that has quietly lost momentum, you are not alone — and more importantly, you are not behind.

What you actually need is not a new goal. You need a new target for the same goal you already set.

That distinction matters more than it sounds. Michele O'Neil, a digital business coach who built a multi-million dollar online business without ever sending a cold DM, calls this the realignment. And she makes the case that the month of June is the single best time to do it.

Here is why the timing works: if you realign right now, you still have all of July, August, September, October, November, and December — six full months — to finish what you started. The goal does not die in June. It gets relaunched.

---

## Why You Feel Behind (And Why That Feeling Is Lying to You)

The habit most entrepreneurs are stuck in is not failing — it is goal-switching.

Something gets hard. Progress slows. The excitement of January fades. So the response is to set a new goal, start fresh, find a new system, try a new approach. It feels productive. It is actually the reason nothing ever gets finished.

Michele is direct about this: never change the goal until you achieve the goal. What you change are the targets — the specific milestones and approaches you use to reach it. The goal itself stays fixed.

When you finally achieve that one goal you refused to quit on, something shifts permanently. You have proven to yourself that you are capable of finishing. That proof compounds. The next goal becomes easier not because the goal is smaller but because you are different — you are now someone who finishes.

That is the version of yourself that becomes unstoppable.

## Clearing the Limiting Beliefs First

Before the three questions, Michele addresses the three most common reasons people talk themselves out of building an online business. All three are false.

The first is tech. Michele makes six figures a month and does not like tech, does not want to learn tech, and has built her systems around avoiding it. If that is the wall stopping you, it is not a real wall.

The second is personality. Michele is an extreme introvert. She built her business without cold outreach, without DMs, without working a room. The idea that you have to be loud, charismatic, or socially tireless to build an online income is simply not accurate.

The third is audience size. She started from zero on TikTok in September 2022 — complete beginner, no following, no idea what she was doing. The follower count did not build her business. The system did.

Remove those three objections from your thinking and a very different set of possibilities opens up.

## The Framework: Three Questions That Actually Move the Needle

Michele's realignment process comes down to three questions. They take less than an hour to work through and they will show you exactly where your next six months need to go.

*Question One: What worked?*

Most people skip this entirely because they are so focused on what is not working that they cannot see what already is. Michele pushes back hard on this tendency.

If you posted content and got any views — five, fifty, five hundred — your content is working. It just needs to work better. That is a completely different problem than content that is not working at all, and it has a completely different solution.

Stack your wins. Write them down. What you focus on, you get more of. When you identify what has already been working since January, you now have something to do more of — and more of what already works is exactly how momentum builds.

A 7-second video Michele filmed in Costa Rica generated $8,000 in sales. She knows that because she tracked it. She knew it worked, so she did more of it. That is the entire model.

*Question Two: What did not work?*

Here is where honesty matters. But Michele draws a careful line between what genuinely failed and what simply needed more consistency, more intention, or more repetition.

What did not work was probably not your business model. What did not work was likely the inconsistency. The stinking thinking. The days you did not look at your goal. The weeks you spent consuming instead of executing.

If you got one lead since January, what you are doing is working. Figure out what you said or did to get that one lead. Say it — or do it — a hundred thousand more times.

She is not being glib about that. She genuinely believes, based on her own data and the results of her students, that people dramatically over-complicate what it takes to build an online income. The system is simple. The hard part is staying in it long enough to let it work.

*Question Three: What did you never start?*

This is the most important question and the most uncomfortable one.

How many goals did you set in January that you never actually began? Not goals you tried and struggled with — goals you set and then simply never took the first step toward.

This is where the realignment lives. Not in optimizing what you are already doing. In finally starting the thing you put off.

## Build Your Business Off Data, Not Emotion

The reason Michele can make decisions quickly and confidently — the reason she is not paralyzed by algorithm changes or slow weeks — is because she tracks everything.

She spends 10 minutes per week writing down her data: how many leads, how many sales, what she is going to do differently next week. That is it. Ten minutes. From that practice, she is never operating off of hope or frustration. She is operating off of information.

She describes the alternative — posting and praying, hoping the algorithm favors you, making decisions based on how you feel about your content — as the source of most of the pressure and anxiety entrepreneurs carry. There is no peace in that approach. There is no strategy in it either.

Data removes the emotion from the equation. When a video underperforms, it is a data point, not a verdict on your worth. When something works, you know exactly why, and you do more of it.

This is how she built a multi-million dollar business without sending a single DM. Not hustle. Not volume. Not grinding through cold outreach. A system, tracked consistently, adjusted based on what the numbers actually said.

## The Leadership Lid

One more concept Michele shares that is worth sitting with.

There is a John C. Maxwell book called The Law of the Lid. The premise is that your results will never exceed your leadership level. It is not about strategy, not about the platform, not about your niche. It is about whether you have done the internal work to lead yourself well.

If the idea of being fully responsible for your income feels like pressure rather than peace, Michele says that is a signal — not to quit, but to invest in your leadership development. The strategies are learnable. The mindset that sustains them through the hard months is the real work.

Winners never quit on the goal. Quitters never win.

The only reason you will not reach your goals in 2026 is because you stop before you get there.
