What if the thing holding your business back is not your content strategy?

What if it is not your offer, your niche, your posting frequency, or your follower count?

What if the missing piece is something almost no one in the online business space is talking about — and yet it is the exact reason some entrepreneurs quietly build multi-million dollar businesses while others stay stuck producing content that goes nowhere?

According to digital business coach Michele O'Neil, the answer is community. And the distinction she draws between what most business owners are doing and what actually works is worth understanding before you spend another hour creating content no one is engaging with.

---

## People Do Not Need More Information

This is the insight that changes everything once it lands.

Your potential customers are not suffering from a lack of information. They have access to more content, more tutorials, more tips, and more strategies than they could consume in a lifetime. Information is not scarce. Transformation is.

What people actually crave — and what they will pay for, return to, and refer others toward — is connection. They want to feel seen. They want to feel heard. They want to be part of something where their questions get answered, their wins get celebrated, and their struggles get met with real support rather than another video to watch alone.

When a business owner creates that kind of environment, something shifts. The relationship between the business and its customers stops being transactional and starts becoming something people are genuinely loyal to.

That loyalty is where recurring income lives.

---

## What a Community Actually Did for One Business

Drawing from insights shared by Michele O'Neil — a digital business coach whose Skool community became the number one ranked business community on the platform — the impact of building a community space for customers and potential customers was immediate and compounding.

When she launched her business in late 2022, she created a community alongside it — not after success, but from the very beginning. The reason was straightforward: she wanted to understand how her customers were doing and how she could serve them better.

What happened next shaped everything.

Her customers started telling her what they actually wanted. They showed her, through their questions and conversations, what problems they were still facing, what language resonated, and what gaps in the market remained wide open. That feedback loop directly improved her email campaigns, her social content, and her offers.

The result: email open rates above 60 percent across a list of tens of thousands of subscribers. Not because of a clever subject line formula — because she had built genuine trust with people who felt known and valued inside her community.

That is the compounding effect most content strategies can never replicate.

---

## The False Belief Worth Destroying

Here is a limiting belief that keeps most entrepreneurs from even starting a community:

"I need a big audience first."

This is backwards. A community is how you build the audience — not the reward you receive after you already have one.

Michele launched with no following, no reputation in the space, and no roadmap. She built the community first and let the community do what communities naturally do: create connection, generate conversation, and attract more people who resonate with what is being built.

Size is not the goal. Engagement is. A community of five hundred highly engaged people who trust you, learn from you, and buy from you is worth more than fifty thousand passive followers who scroll past your content without a second thought.

The number one ranked community on a major platform is not number one because it is the biggest. It is number one because it is the most engaged. That distinction matters enormously.

---

## Your Community Works When You Do Not

This is the time-freedom piece that most online business builders are chasing without realizing how close they already are to it.

When a community is built and active, it does not stop when you step away. Members answer each other's questions. Conversations continue overnight. New people discover the community through search and referral. Relationships form without your direct involvement in every exchange.

This is fundamentally different from a social media feed, which goes cold the moment you stop posting. A community has its own momentum. It is an asset that appreciates over time rather than a content hamster wheel that demands constant output just to maintain visibility.

For a parent entrepreneur managing a household, a side business, and everything in between, this distinction is not just nice to have. It is the difference between a business that is sustainable and one that burns you out before it ever reaches its potential.

---

## A 30-Minute Daily Outreach Strategy That Adds 40 New Connections Per Day

One of the most practical insights Michele shares with her students is this: she spends just 30 minutes per day on outreach, and that single practice consistently brings an average of 40 new people — customers and interested prospects — into her community daily.

Not 30 minutes of DMs. Not 30 minutes of cold pitching. A targeted, intentional outreach practice built around serving and connecting, not selling.

The principle behind it is simple. When you lead with value, when you are genuinely trying to help people solve a real problem, the conversion happens naturally. You are not chasing people. You are creating a space people want to walk into.

That kind of daily consistency, compounded over months and years, is how a community goes from a handful of members to tens of thousands — and how a business goes from scraping for sales to generating recurring income that does not depend on any single piece of content going viral.

---

## The Bottom Line for Parent Entrepreneurs

If you have been grinding on content — posting every day, tweaking your hooks, chasing the algorithm — and wondering why the results do not match the effort, this framework offers a different answer.

Stop asking how to get more eyeballs on your content. Start asking how to create a space where the people who already found you feel compelled to stay, engage, and bring others in.

Community is not a feature you add to your business after it is already successful. It is the foundation that makes sustained success possible in the first place.

People do not need more content. They need a place to belong.
