Summer is in full swing in Omaha, and there is no shortage of ways to get your family out of the house and making memories together. Whether you are looking for faith-based activities, free outdoor fun, or entertaining festivals, this week has something for every age and budget. Here are the 11 best picks for families this week.

## EDITOR'S TOP PICK

**Rainforest Falls VBS at King of Kings Fremont Campus — June 22-26, 9:00-11:30 AM**

If you have not already heard about Vacation Bible School this summer, Rainforest Falls at King of Kings is the crown jewel of Omaha VBS season. This week-long adventure is designed for kids ages 4-12 and blends Bible stories, games, crafts, music, and worship into one cohesive program rooted in faith. The Fremont campus runs June 22-26, with morning sessions from 9 to 11:30 AM. Registration is free, and it is one of the most highly rated VBS programs in the Omaha area. Parents often comment on how engaged their kids are and how much Bible truth sticks with them all summer long.

What makes this stand out: The program is intentionally designed around a theme (Rainforest Falls) that captures kids' imaginations while teaching them what it means to be rooted in a relationship with God. The balance of fun and faith is exactly what Omaha families appreciate.

## FAITH AND COMMUNITY

**St. Luke Lutheran Church VBS 2026 — June 28-July 2, 6:00-8:30 PM**

If mornings do not work for your family, St. Luke Lutheran Church is offering VBS in the evening. June 28 through July 2 from 6:00 to 8:30 PM, this week-long program is free and open to all ages. Evening VBS is a great option for families with varying schedules or who want a wind-down activity after dinner. Registration is open now.

**King of Kings Millard Summer Park Meet Up — Wednesday, June 25, Afternoon**

Looking for a casual way to connect with your faith community? King of Kings Millard is hosting a free summer park meet up on Wednesday afternoon at their playground. Bring the kids, let them play, and chat with other families in your community. No registration required — just show up and enjoy the outdoors.

## FREE FAMILY FUN

**Omaha Parks Spraygrounds & Splash Pads — Daily Through Labor Day, 9 AM - 8 PM**

On a hot summer day, nothing beats a free splash pad. Omaha has multiple spraygrounds and splash pads throughout the city, all free and open daily from 9 AM to 8 PM through Labor Day. These are perfect for cooling off, burning energy, and enjoying outdoor water play without spending a dime. Bring a towel and sunscreen, and let the kids run wild.

**Kids Bowl Free Summer Program — All Summer, Multiple Locations**

Did you know you can register your kids for two free games of bowling every day throughout the summer? The Kids Bowl Free program is running at participating bowling alleys across Omaha. Shoe rental may apply, but the bowling itself is completely free. It is a great way to beat the heat indoors and have structured summer fun.

**Nebraska Great Park Pursuit — All Summer, 20 Parks Across Nebraska**

Turn your family outings into an adventure with the Nebraska Great Park Pursuit. Visit any of 20 participating parks across Nebraska, make pencil rubbings of special markers, and enter drawings to win outdoor recreation packages worth up to $1,500. It is free to participate and gives your family an excuse to explore hidden corners of Nebraska you may have never visited.

**Community Fishing Events — Ongoing Through September, Various Parks**

Omaha Parks & Recreation is hosting free community fishing events throughout the summer at various locations. Whether your kids are seasoned anglers or complete beginners, these events are welcoming and educational. It is a peaceful way to introduce kids to outdoor recreation and enjoy time together as a family.

## TICKETED SHOWS WORTH THE COST

**Mary Poppins at Omaha Community Playhouse — Select Dates Through August**

If you are looking for an indoor entertainment experience that the whole family will remember, Mary Poppins at the Omaha Community Playhouse is practically perfect. This beloved story comes to life on stage with professional actors, beautiful sets, and music that will have your kids talking about it for weeks. Ticket prices vary, but special family performances often have discounted rates. Check their website for exact show dates and times.

## PLAN AHEAD FOR LATER THIS SUMMER

**Nebraska Passport Program — All Summer**

The Nebraska Passport Program is a statewide scavenger hunt adventure where families visit up to 20 popular attractions, restaurants, historical sites, and locally owned businesses. Participants collect passport stamps at each location and enter drawings for prizes. While it does have some costs associated with visiting attractions, it is a fun way to explore and discover hidden gems across the state.

## CONCLUSION

This week in Omaha offers a beautiful mix of free outdoor water play, faith-based programming, community connection, and ticketed entertainment for families who want something special. Whether you spend the week at splash pads, attend VBS, or take in a show, your family will be making memories and enjoying the best of summer in Omaha.

The best part? Most of these activities are free or low-cost, which means you can do multiple things throughout the week without breaking the budget. Summer in Omaha is short, so get out there and enjoy it while it lasts.
