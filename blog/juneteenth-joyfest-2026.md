# Juneteenth JoyFest 2026: A Free Family Celebration of Freedom & Culture in Omaha

This weekend, Omaha comes alive with the **6th Annual Juneteenth JoyFest** — a vibrant, FREE family festival celebrating 161 years of freedom, Black artistry, and community pride. If you're looking for **things to do in Omaha with kids** that are meaningful, fun, and cost nothing, this is it.

## What You Need to Know

**Event:** Juneteenth JoyFest 2026  
**When:** Saturday-Sunday, June 20-21, 2026 (Main event Saturday 2-10 PM)  
**Where:** The Union for Contemporary Art, Omaha, NE  
**Cost:** FREE (donations welcome)  
**Best For:** All ages — families, kids, teens, grandparents, everyone

## What to Expect: Movement, Migration & Celebration

This year's theme is **Movement & Migration** — exploring the journeys, resilience, and strength of the Black community. But don't let that intimidate you. This festival is joyful, colorful, alive, and absolutely family-friendly.

Here's what you'll find:

**Live Music & Entertainment**
Local and regional artists perform throughout the day. Expect everything from soul to hip-hop, spoken word to gospel. The energy is contagious, and your kids will be dancing.

**Local Black Artists & Vendors**
Support Omaha's Black-owned businesses and artists. Browse handmade crafts, art installations, jewelry, and unique goods you won't find anywhere else.

**Food Vendors**
Come hungry. You'll find authentic soul food, Caribbean cuisine, BBQ, sweets, and more. This is peak food festival season, and Juneteenth JoyFest delivers.

**Kids Activities & Family Zones**
There's a dedicated family area with interactive activities, games, and workshops designed for children. Your little ones get to celebrate, create, and learn about Black culture and history in a fun, welcoming space.

**Community Celebration**
Juneteenth marks June 19, 1865 — when enslaved people in Galveston, Texas finally learned they were free. It's a day of liberation, reflection, and joy. At JoyFest, you'll witness real community — people gathering to honor freedom, support each other, and build something meaningful together.

## Why You Should Go

If you're a parent in Omaha searching for **free family events** that matter, this is your answer. Too many "family activities" are just sugar-coated commercialism. Juneteenth JoyFest is different. It's real. It teaches kids about history, celebrates Black excellence, supports local artists and businesses, and fills your weekend with actual joy.

Plus? It costs nothing. No ticket booths, no hidden fees. Just show up and celebrate.

## Pro Tips for Families

- **Bring a blanket or chairs** if you want to watch performances from the grass
- **Sunscreen and water** — it's June in Nebraska. The sun is real.
- **Cash for food vendors** — bring extra if you want to support local artists
- **Start early** — arrive by 3 PM to beat the crowds and catch the afternoon performances
- **Plan for bathroom breaks** — it's outdoors, so facilities may have lines
