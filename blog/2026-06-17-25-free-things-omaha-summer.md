## Free Splash Pads and Water Fun in Omaha

Omaha's city spraygrounds are free, and for 2026 they are listed as open daily from 9 a.m. to 8 p.m., May 24 through August 31, weather permitting.

### 1. Miller Park Sprayground

Miller Park is one of the big wins for free water fun in Omaha. It has a Viking theme, 37 spray features, a dump bucket, and a huge play area. Bring towels, snacks, sunscreen, and assume your kids will not want to leave fast.

### 2. Benson Park Sprayground

Benson Park is a great pick for younger kids. The sprayground has 29 water features with fun cattails, insects, and flowers. There are also shaded spots nearby, which matters when the Omaha heat is doing the most.

### 3. Fontenelle Park Sprayground

Fontenelle Park is a solid toddler-friendly option. You will find dumping bells, spinners, spray flowers, interactive ground sprays, and low water jellies that are easier for little ones. It is simple, free, and a nice way to cool off without turning it into a whole production.

### 4. Gene Leahy Mall and Heartland of America Park Water Features

Downtown Omaha gives you another easy free water stop. The RiverFront includes Gene Leahy Mall, Heartland of America Park, and Lewis and Clark Landing, with free events and activities for all ages, including playgrounds, art carts, board game carts, classes, and movie nights.

## Free Parks, Trails, and Playgrounds in Omaha

Sometimes the best plan is the easiest one. Pack snacks. Fill the water bottles. Let the kids climb, walk, run, roll down a hill, or look for turtles.

### 5. Gene Leahy Mall at The RiverFront

This is one of the best free things to do with kids in Omaha, period. You get giant slides, a big lawn, public art, a state-of-the-art playground, and easy downtown energy. It is free, open daily, and honestly feels like Omaha showing off in the best way.

### 6. Bob Kerrey Pedestrian Bridge

![Bob Kerrey Pedestrian Bridge spanning the Missouri River between Omaha and Council Bluffs](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/89/Bob_Kerrey_Pedestrian_Bridge_alt_%28cropped%29.jpg)
*Walk across two states for free — the Bob Kerrey Pedestrian Bridge connects Omaha to Council Bluffs across the Missouri River. Photo: Wikimedia Commons (CC0)*

Walk, scooter, or stroller across the Missouri River. The kids can stand in Nebraska and Iowa at the same time, which is one of those simple Omaha memories that somehow always works. The bridge connects Omaha and Council Bluffs and gives you great river views for free.

### 7. Heron Haven

Need something quieter? Heron Haven is a spring-fed wetland and wildlife sanctuary in Omaha. Trails are open to the public from sunup to sundown, and kids can look for turtles, frogs, birds, and other wildlife from the trails and boardwalk areas.

### 8. Zorinsky Lake

Zorinsky is a classic west Omaha pick. You have got paved trails, fishing spots, playgrounds, and plenty of room to move. It works for strollers, scooters, bikes, and kids who just need to burn off some energy.

### 9. Standing Bear Lake

Standing Bear Lake is another easy outdoor day. It has a playground, fishing, no-wake boating, and hiking and biking trails. It is a nice pick when you want space, water views, and a slower morning.

### 10. Chalco Hills Recreation Area

Chalco Hills is a great free family stop southwest of Omaha. You can hike, bike, fish at Wehrspann Lake, picnic, and take in prairie views. The area has more than nine miles of trails, so you can make it as short or long as your kids can handle.

### 11. Memorial Park and Rose Garden

Memorial Park is simple, pretty, and always a good idea. Bring a picnic. Toss a frisbee. Let the kids roll down the hill. Then walk through the rose garden for a slower moment before you head home. The park is free and open daily.

### 12. Hanscom Park

Hanscom Park is one of Omaha's oldest parks, and it is still worth a visit. You will find open green space, a playground, walking paths, a lagoon, and plenty of shade. It is also right near the Gerald R. Ford Birthsite and Gardens, so you can pair two free stops in one easy trip.

## Free Museums and Hands-On Learning

Hot day? Rainy day? Somebody needs air conditioning? These free Omaha activities give kids something to do and give parents a little break.

### 13. Joslyn Art Museum

![Joslyn Art Museum exterior in Omaha Nebraska](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a2/Joslyn_Art_Museum_Omaha_Nebraska.jpg)
*Joslyn Art Museum — free general admission, always. One of the best free indoor stops in Omaha for families. Photo: Wikimedia Commons (CC BY 2.0)*

Joslyn Art Museum is one of the best free museums in Omaha. General admission is free to everyone, always. The museum is open Tuesday through Sunday, closed Mondays, and open late on Thursdays. Kids 12 and under are also free for ticketed exhibitions.

### 14. Do Space at Omaha Public Library

Do Space is now part of Omaha Public Library, and it is still a great free tech stop for curious kids. It is designed for people of all ages and experience levels, with spaces that support kids, teens, adults, and different learning needs. Think coding, creating, building, and trying something new without paying for another camp.

### 15. Union Pacific Railroad Museum

If your kid loves trains, this is the one. The Union Pacific Railroad Museum in Council Bluffs is free and open to the public. It is just across the river, and it makes an easy Omaha-area indoor adventure.

### 16. Hot Shops Art Center

Hot Shops is one of those places that feels like a hidden gem, even though it is right here. Admission is free, always. You can walk through working artist studios, see art in progress, and sometimes catch glassblowing, pottery, blacksmithing, or other "wait, they make that here?" moments.

### 17. Mormon Trail Center at Winter Quarters

The Mormon Trail Center is free and family-friendly. Kids can learn about pioneer history through exhibits about wagons, handcarts, travel, and life on the trail. It is a visitors' center with exhibits about the westward migration, and it is free to visit.

### 18. Gerald R. Ford Birthsite and Gardens

This is a short, easy, free stop. The gardens honor President Gerald R. Ford, who was born in Omaha. It is free, open daily, and pairs well with Hanscom Park if you want to make one little loop out of it.

## Free Events and Summer Programs in Omaha

Free events are where Omaha really starts to feel full in the summer. Here are a few easy ones to keep on your radar.

### 19. Jazz on the Green

Jazz on the Green is a classic Omaha summer event. In 2026, the free concerts run Thursdays from July 9 through August 13 at Turner Park in Midtown Crossing. The lawn opens at 5 p.m., preshow starts at 6:30 p.m., and the music starts at 7:30 p.m. Bring a blanket, pack a picnic, and let the kids dance up front.

### 20. Saturdays at Stinson Park

Aksarben Village hosts free Saturday night concerts at Stinson Park during the summer. It is an easy family setup because there is music, green space, nearby food, and room for kids to move around without needing a formal plan.

### 21. Benson First Friday

Benson First Friday is a free monthly art walk in the Benson Creative District. Go earlier in the evening if you are bringing kids. You can check out art, local vendors, music, and the neighborhood vibe before it turns more grown-up later at night.

### 22. Omaha Farmers Markets

Yes, you may buy peaches. Or pastries. Or flowers. But walking the Omaha Farmers Market is free. The Old Market runs Saturdays from 8 a.m. to 12:30 p.m., and Aksarben Village runs Sundays from 9 a.m. to 1 p.m. It is stroller-friendly, lively, and a fun way to get out before the day gets too hot.

### 23. Omaha Public Library Summer Reading Program

This is one of the easiest free summer wins. Omaha Public Library's 2026 Summer Reading Program gives babies, toddlers, kids, and teens two free books when they register. Kids can also complete reading or activity goals for prizes and event rewards.

### 24. Free Outdoor Movie Nights

Free outdoor movie nights pop up around Omaha all summer. The RiverFront offers free events for all ages, and Omaha-area calendars list free outdoor movies with yard games, themed activities, and family-friendly screenings. Bring chairs, blankets, snacks, and bug spray. That is the whole plan.

### 25. Kids Bowl Free

This one is a lifesaver on rainy days, smoky days, or those "it is too hot to exist outside" days. Through Kids Bowl Free, registered kids can get free bowling at participating Omaha-area lanes. Some locations offer two free games per day, but shoe rental may not be included, so check the details before you go.
