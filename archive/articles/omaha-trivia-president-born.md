Quick, name the city where President Gerald Ford was born. Most people guess somewhere in Michigan, since that is where he grew up and built his political career. But the real answer is one of the most fun pieces of trivia in the whole country: the 38th President of the United States was born right here in Omaha, Nebraska. And the story only gets more surprising from there.

Here is the part that stumps even history buffs. When he was born in Omaha on July 14, 1913, the future president was not named Gerald Ford at all. He came into the world as Leslie Lynch King Jr., born in a grand house on the corner where the Gerald R. Ford Birthsite and Gardens stands today. His name changed later in childhood after his mother, Dorothy, moved away and remarried a kind man named Gerald Ford, whose name the boy eventually took as his own. So the president America came to know as Gerald R. Ford began his life as a baby boy in Omaha with an entirely different name.

That twist makes for one of the best trivia questions you can ask: which U.S. president was born under a different name in Omaha, Nebraska? Almost nobody gets it right, and almost everybody is delighted to learn the answer.

Today, Omaha proudly remembers its presidential connection. The original house where he was born is no longer standing, but the site at 32nd and Woolworth has been turned into the lovely Gerald R. Ford Birthsite and Gardens, a peaceful spot with a memorial, a rose garden, and a model of the original home. For visitors, it is a charming and unexpected stop, a chance to stand on the very ground where an American president entered the world. It is the kind of place that makes you realize how much surprising history is tucked into this friendly Heartland city.

This little-known fact is a perfect example of what makes Omaha such a fun place to explore. The city is full of these delightful surprises, stories that make you say, wait, that happened here? A president, a Hollywood legend, the most famous investor in the world, all with Omaha roots. For a city that often flies under the radar, Omaha has quietly played a starring role in American history again and again.

If you love this kind of trivia, Omaha is the perfect place to dig up more of it.
