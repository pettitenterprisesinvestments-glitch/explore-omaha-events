## The Conversation Your Marriage Has Been Waiting For

You talk to your spouse every day.

*"Did you pick up the kids?"* *"What do you want for dinner?"* *"Did you see that email from the school?"*

Logistics. Schedules. The endless coordination of two lives running parallel.

But when was the last time you had a conversation that actually *meant something*?

Not an argument. Not a check-in. A real conversation — the kind where you walked away feeling closer, more known, more seen.

If you're having to think about it... that's the point.

---

## Why We Stop Talking (Really Talking)

It's not that couples stop caring. It's that the conversations that used to come naturally — in the early days, when everything was new — get replaced by the business of running a household.

And over time, without realizing it, you can end up living with your best friend and feeling strangely alone.

This isn't a crisis. It's just drift. And drift can be reversed — not with a therapy session necessarily, but sometimes with a single honest conversation at the right moment.

---

## 10 Questions Worth Asking Your Spouse Tonight

These aren't trick questions. They're not therapy homework. They're just invitations to be known a little better.

Pick one. Light a candle. Put the phones face down. And actually listen to the answer.

**1. What's something you've been wanting to tell me but haven't found the right moment?**

**2. What do I do that makes you feel most loved — and what do I sometimes miss?**

**3. What's a dream you had when you were younger that you've never talked to me about?**

**4. Is there anything in our life right now that's draining you that I might not know about?**

**5. What's something you're proud of yourself for lately?**

**6. If we could completely change one thing about how we spend our time together, what would it be?**

**7. What's something you've always wanted to try but felt too embarrassed to bring up?**

**8. When do you feel most like yourself — and do I make space for that version of you?**

**9. What would "a really good year for us" look like?**

**10. Is there anything you need from me right now that I haven't been giving?**

---

## A Few Ground Rules

These questions only work if you actually *listen* — not to respond, not to defend yourself, but to understand.

If your spouse says something that surprises you or stings a little, your first job isn't to explain yourself. It's to say: *"Tell me more about that."*

The goal isn't to solve anything in one night. The goal is to make your spouse feel safe enough to tell you the truth — and to remind them that you genuinely want to know.

---

## The Smallest Possible Step

You don't need a weekend getaway or a marriage retreat (though those are great if you can do them).

You need twenty minutes. A quiet room. One question.

That's it. That's the whole prescription.

The couples who stay close over decades aren't the ones who never drift — they're the ones who notice when they have, and choose to row back toward each other.
