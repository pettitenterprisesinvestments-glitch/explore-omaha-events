Here is a trivia question that almost nobody guesses correctly: where would you find the largest indoor desert in the entire world? Not Arizona, not Nevada, not the Sahara. The answer is Omaha, Nebraska, where one of the most jaw-dropping attractions you can imagine sits under a giant glass dome at Omaha's Henry Doorly Zoo and Aquarium.

It is called the Desert Dome, and it lives up to every part of that name. Step inside and you are transported into a sprawling indoor desert complete with rolling sand dunes, towering rock canyons, cacti, and the plants and animals of deserts from around the globe. It is so large and so realistic that it holds the world record as the largest indoor desert on the planet. And it is all housed beneath the largest glazed geodesic dome in the world, an architectural marvel that has become an instantly recognizable part of the Omaha skyline.

The Desert Dome opened in 2002 and quickly became one of the zoo's signature attractions. But the wonders do not stop at the sand. Beneath the desert, in a hidden lower level called Kingdoms of the Night, visitors can explore the largest indoor nocturnal exhibit in the world, a mysterious realm of caves, swamps, and creatures of the dark. So in a single building, Omaha manages to hold not one but two remarkable world records, stacked literally on top of each other.

And here is the thing: the Desert Dome is just one attraction inside what is widely considered one of the best zoos in the entire world. Omaha's Henry Doorly Zoo is famous far beyond Nebraska, regularly ranked among the top zoos anywhere, and it is packed with other record-setting wonders. There is the Lied Jungle, one of the largest indoor rainforests in the world, where you can walk through a steamy jungle full of monkeys and waterfalls. There is a vast aquarium, a butterfly pavilion, and so much more. Families can easily spend an entire day here and still not see it all.

That is what makes this such a delightful piece of Omaha trivia. People often picture Nebraska as flat prairie and cornfields, never imagining that one of its cities is home to a world-class zoo with a record-breaking indoor desert, a record-breaking rainforest, and a record-breaking nocturnal kingdom all under one roof. It is exactly the kind of surprise that makes visiting Omaha so much fun.

For families planning a trip, the Henry Doorly Zoo alone is worth the journey. It is consistently ranked as one of the top attractions in the Midwest, the kind of place that turns a weekend getaway into an unforgettable adventure for kids and grown-ups alike. Walking from a desert to a rainforest to the ocean depths, all in a single afternoon, is an experience you simply cannot get just anywhere.

So the next time someone asks where to find the world's largest indoor desert, you will have a fun answer and a great reason to plan a visit.
