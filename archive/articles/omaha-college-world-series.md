Every June, while much of the country is firing up the grill and heading to the lake, the eyes of the entire baseball world turn to one place: Omaha, Nebraska. For two unforgettable weeks each summer, the best college baseball teams in America converge on the city for the Men's College World Series, and Omaha transforms into the undisputed capital of the sport. What many visitors do not realize, as they fill the stadium and pack the surrounding streets, is that this beloved tradition almost never happened here at all.

The College World Series did not begin in Omaha. The very first one was played in 1947 in Kalamazoo, Michigan, and after another year there it moved to Wichita, Kansas. It was a young, money-losing event still searching for a permanent home. But a group of determined Omaha boosters saw something everyone else had missed. They traveled to the 1949 series in Wichita, made their pitch to NCAA officials, and convinced them to give their city a chance. In 1950, the College World Series came to Omaha for the first time, and it never left.

It would be a lovely story if success had been instant, but it was not. For much of its first decade in Omaha, the tournament actually lost money, year after year. A lesser city might have given up. Omaha did the opposite. The community rallied around the event, volunteers poured in their time, local leaders refused to let it fail, and slowly the College World Series put down roots so deep that today it is almost impossible to imagine it anywhere else. That stubborn, hardworking loyalty is about as Omaha as it gets.

For sixty seasons, the games were played at the legendary Johnny Rosenblatt Stadium, a ballpark that generations of fans came to love and that still lives warmly in the city's memory. Then, in 2011, the series moved downtown to a gleaming new ballpark, now known as Charles Schwab Field Omaha, just steps from the riverfront and the Old Market. The move marked a new chapter, but the heart of the event never changed. The College World Series remains a family-friendly celebration of young athletes, hometown pride, and the simple joy of summer baseball.

What makes the College World Series so special is not only the baseball, though the baseball is wonderful. It is the way the whole city leans in. Neighborhoods fill with fans from across the country. Families make it an annual tradition, passing the love of the games down to their kids. The atmosphere is wholesome, electric, and unmistakably American, the kind of event that reminds you why people fall in love with a sport in the first place.

And here is the best part for anyone reading this from out of town: you are absolutely invited. There may be no better time to experience Omaha than in June, when the city is alive with the energy of the College World Series and everything around it. You can catch a game, explore the historic Old Market a short walk away, stroll the beautiful riverfront, and discover a city that knows how to throw a party while keeping it family-friendly.

So whether you are a die-hard baseball fan or simply looking for a memorable summer trip, consider putting Omaha on your list.
