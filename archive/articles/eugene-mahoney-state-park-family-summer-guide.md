Sometimes the best family adventure is not a far-flung vacation but a simple drive down the interstate to a place that has a little bit of everything. That is the magic of Eugene T. Mahoney State Park, a beautifully kept gem tucked into the wooded hills between Omaha and Lincoln, just off Interstate 80 near Ashland. It sits barely half an hour from Omaha, which means you can be splashing, hiking, or paddling within minutes of leaving home, and that easy access makes it one of the most beloved family destinations in all of Nebraska.

What sets Mahoney apart is the sheer variety packed into one welcoming park. In the warm months, the family aquatic center is a highlight, with water slides and a splash-friendly pool that gives kids hours of happy, sun-soaked fun. Down at the lagoon you can rent paddle boats and glide across the calm water together, and anglers young and old can drop a line and try their luck. Miles of shaded hiking and nature trails wind through the timber, perfect for a family walk where the kids can hunt for wildlife and wildflowers. And rising above it all is the park's iconic observation tower, where a climb to the top rewards you with sweeping views of the Platte River valley that make little ones feel like they can see the whole world.

The wonderful thing about Mahoney is how naturally it fits any kind of family day. You can come for a few hours of swimming and a picnic, or you can settle in for a full day of trails, mini golf, playgrounds, and exploring. And for families who want to stretch the magic into an overnight, the park offers cozy cabins and a lodge, turning a simple outing into a peaceful little getaway without the long drive or the big expense. Many families make it an annual tradition, a place they return to season after season as the children grow.

This kind of outing is a gift for families because it trades screens for fresh air and gives everyone, from toddlers to grandparents, something to enjoy at their own pace. There is room to roam, plenty of shade for resting, and the gentle, unhurried rhythm of a day spent outdoors together. It is the sort of place where a child learns to bait a hook, conquers a climbing wall of nerves at the top of the tower, or simply falls asleep happy and sun-tired on the ride home.

A few practical notes will help you plan. Nebraska state parks require a vehicle entry permit, available as a daily pass or an annual one, so be sure to pick that up when you arrive or online beforehand. The aquatic center and certain seasonal activities run on summer schedules and can fill up on hot weekends, so arriving early is wise. Pack swimsuits and towels, sunscreen and hats, water bottles, and a picnic or snacks, along with comfortable shoes for the trails. If you plan to stay overnight in a cabin, those book up quickly in summer, so reserve ahead.

If you love the idea of building a summer full of easy local adventures, Mahoney pairs beautifully with our other ideas for getting the family outdoors, including our guide to a <a href='/article/midwest-road-trip-family-omaha'>family road trip around Omaha</a>. Make it a standing summer tradition, and watch how quickly it becomes one of your children's favorite places in the world.

For current hours, the aquatic center schedule, entry permit details, and cabin reservations, visit Nebraska Game and Parks at <a href='https://outdoornebraska.gov'>outdoornebraska.gov</a> before you head out. Adventure, it turns out, is closer than you think.
