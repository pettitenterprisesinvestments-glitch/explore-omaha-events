Tucked along the bluffs of the Missouri River sits one of Omaha's loveliest surprises, a sprawling botanical paradise that bursts with color from spring through fall and dazzles with lights in winter. Lauritzen Gardens, Omaha's botanical center, covers a hundred acres of gardens, woodlands, and walking paths, and it has quietly become one of the most beautiful places to spend a day in the entire region. What surprises most visitors is how young this gem actually is.

Unlike many of the great botanical gardens of the world, which trace their roots back centuries, Lauritzen Gardens is a thoroughly modern achievement, a gift that the people of Omaha gave themselves. The gardens first opened in 1994 as the Omaha Botanical Gardens, beginning the patient, hopeful work of turning bluff-side land into something extraordinary. In 2001, a beautiful new visitor center opened and the gardens were renamed Lauritzen Gardens, in honor of the Lauritzen family, whose generosity helped make the vision real. From there, it only kept growing.

And grow it has. In 2014, the gardens added the stunning Marjorie K. Daugherty Conservatory, a glass jewel box where lush tropical and desert plants thrive no matter the Nebraska weather outside, so you can wander through a rainforest in the middle of a snowy January. More recently, in 2025, the gardens opened Sofia's Play Garden, a delightful space designed to let children explore, climb, and fall in love with nature. With each addition, Lauritzen Gardens has become more of a destination and more of a treasure.

A visit is a feast for the senses. You can stroll through formal gardens bursting with roses, wander woodland trails, admire the changing seasonal displays, and let the kids loose in spaces built just for them. Families especially love the charming model railroad garden, where miniature trains wind through tiny landscapes. And in the depths of winter, the gardens transform into a glittering wonderland of holiday lights that has become a cherished Omaha tradition, drawing crowds bundled up against the cold to walk among the glow.

What makes Lauritzen Gardens so special is the way it blends beauty, learning, and peace. It is a place to slow down. On a warm afternoon, you can find people sketching among the flowers, families picnicking, couples strolling hand in hand, and children crouched low to watch a butterfly. In a busy world, it offers something increasingly rare: a hundred acres of calm, cultivated beauty, open to everyone.

There is a lovely lesson in Lauritzen Gardens, too. It is proof that a great thing does not have to be old to be magnificent, and that a community willing to dream and to give can create lasting beauty in a single generation. A patch of riverside bluff became, in just a few decades, one of the finest gardens in the Midwest, all because the people of Omaha decided their city deserved it.

So when you plan your trip to Omaha, set aside an afternoon for Lauritzen Gardens, whatever the season. Wander the paths, breathe in the flowers, bring the kids to play, and discover one of the most beautiful corners of the city.
