Six months after their daughter was born, Sam and Olivia realized they hadn't had a single conversation that wasn't about the baby. Not one. Feeding schedules, diaper counts, who slept worse, whose turn it was. They were a phenomenal parenting team and total strangers as a couple.

One night, both of them exhausted, Olivia started crying, not because anything was wrong with the baby, but because she missed her husband. He was right there beside her, and she missed him. Sam admitted he felt it too. They'd poured so much into being good parents that they'd quietly stopped being partners.

If you have young kids, you know this season. The romance doesn't die in a dramatic blowout. It gets crowded out, one urgent little need at a time, until the marriage is running on fumes and you're too tired to even notice.

Here's the truth that helped them, and it's worth sitting with: the best thing you can do for your children is to protect your marriage. Kids don't actually need parents who sacrifice everything for them. They need to grow up inside the warmth of two people who still love each other. Your relationship is the climate they live in.

So Sam and Olivia got intentional, because in this season, romance doesn't happen by accident anymore. It has to be scheduled, and that's okay.

- **The at-home date.** No babysitter required. Once the baby's down, no phones, no TV, just the two of them, a real dinner, a real conversation. Low effort, surprisingly powerful.
- **The non-baby rule.** For the first twenty minutes of date time, the baby is off-limits as a topic. Hard at first. Essential. You have to remember you're more than co-managers of a small human.
- **Small touches all day.** A hand on the back, a hug in the kitchen, a kiss that lasts a beat longer than usual. Physical affection keeps the wire connected even when you're both running on no sleep.
- **Tag-team rest.** A rested partner is a kinder partner. Trading off so each person gets a real break is one of the most romantic things you can do, even though it looks nothing like romance.

It didn't fix everything overnight. They were still tired. The baby still woke up. But they stopped feeling like ships passing in the night and started feeling like teammates who actually liked each other again.

The parenting years are long and short all at once. Don't put your marriage in storage until the kids move out. The couple you are now is the couple you'll hand back to each other when the house gets quiet again. Tend to them. They're worth it.
