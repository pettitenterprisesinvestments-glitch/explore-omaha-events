Some of the best summer nights in Omaha cost absolutely nothing. On Sunday, July 12, 2026, the City of Omaha Parks and Recreation Department brings its Live on the Lawn free concert series to the Gene Leahy Mall Performance Pavilion in the heart of downtown, and your whole family is invited.

From 7:00 to 9:00 p.m., the green lawn at the pavilion becomes an open-air living room for the city. Local and regional musicians take the stage while families spread out on blankets, kids run barefoot in the grass, and the downtown skyline glows behind it all as the sun goes down. There is no ticket, no reserved seating, and no cover charge. Just bring your lawn chairs or a blanket, find a comfortable spot, and settle in.

Here is what to know before you go. The concert is completely free and open to the public. It takes place at the Gene Leahy Mall Performance Pavilion at 1302 Farnam Street, in the revitalized downtown park that has become one of Omaha's favorite gathering places. Music runs from 7:00 to 9:00 p.m., and the relaxed, family-friendly atmosphere makes it easy to come as you are.

The setting is part of the appeal. The reimagined Gene Leahy Mall is a destination in itself, with rolling lawns, a sculpture garden, a playground, and walking paths that connect to the rest of Omaha's riverfront. Arrive a little early and let the kids burn off energy on the slides and open space before the music starts. Pack a picnic, grab dinner from one of the nearby downtown restaurants, or bring a cooler with snacks and drinks for the family.

Events like this are what make summer in Omaha special. They are wholesome, they are welcoming, and they remind us that some of the richest family memories come from the simplest evenings together under an open sky. For parents looking for an outing that is easy on the budget and good for everyone, a free concert in a beautiful downtown park is hard to beat.

A few simple tips will make the night even better. Bring chairs or a blanket, since seating is open lawn. Pack water and sunscreen for the early evening sun, and a light layer for when it cools down. Plan for parking in the downtown garages and lots near the mall, and give yourself a few extra minutes to walk in. And as always with outdoor events, keep an eye on the forecast.

Live on the Lawn is a wonderful reason to head downtown this July, but it is just one of many free and family-friendly happenings around the metro all summer long.
