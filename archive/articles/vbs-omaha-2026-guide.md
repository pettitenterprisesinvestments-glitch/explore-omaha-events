# Free VBS in Omaha 2026: Your Complete Guide to Vacation Bible School This Summer

Summer is the season Omaha families look forward to all year — and for many, **Vacation Bible School** is the highlight. VBS is one of the best-kept secrets for parents looking for **free, faith-filled, high-energy fun** for their kids. For one week (or one amazing day), children get to dive into Bible stories, make new friends, do crafts, play games, and worship together in a safe, joyful environment.

This summer, Omaha churches are going all-in. We've rounded up **every VBS program in the Omaha metro area for 2026** so you can plan ahead and register before spots fill up!

## 💡 Tips for Omaha VBS Season

**Register early.** The most popular programs — especially King of Kings — fill up fast. Don't wait until the week before!

**Check age requirements.** Most VBS programs start at age 3 or 4 (potty-trained). Make sure your little one meets the minimum before you head out.

**Morning vs. evening sessions.** Several churches offer evening VBS (6–8pm) specifically so working families can participate. Candlewood and Southside Christian are great options for evening programs.

**Volunteer if you can.** Most churches desperately need teen and adult volunteers. It's a great way for older kids and parents to serve and be part of the energy!

**Pack light.** Kids will be active — running games, crafts, outdoor time. Comfortable clothes, closed-toe shoes, and a water bottle are usually all they need.

In a world that feels increasingly loud and complicated, VBS gives kids something simple and profound — a week to slow down, make friends, hear stories about God's love, and be *kids*. And the best part? **Most VBS programs in Omaha are completely free.**

That's the Omaha community at its best — churches opening their doors, volunteers giving their time, and families coming together around what matters most.
