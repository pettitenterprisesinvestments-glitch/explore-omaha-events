She had the corporate job. She had the title. She had the schedule, the commute, and the benefits package.

And she was completely unfulfilled.

Alexis made a decision that a lot of people talk about but very few actually follow through on. She walked away from the traditional 9-to-5 and stepped into the world of sales — not because she had a plan, not because she had training, but because she had a gift she had not yet recognized: the ability to connect with people and communicate her value clearly.

Her first deal closed for $5,000. No script. No formal coaching. No prior experience.

That moment produced a sentence that every parent, every entrepreneur, and every person who has ever felt underpaid for their talent needs to hear:

*"My money piece is my mouthpiece."*

---

## The Skill That Requires No Degree and No Permission

One of the most powerful things about sales as a career path — and as a life skill — is that it operates on a completely different set of rules than the traditional economy.

No credit score required. No degree required. No manager has to approve your raise. You are not trading hours for a salary someone else decided you deserve.

You are paid for what you produce.

In a conversation on the Circle of Greatness podcast with host Nehemiah Davis, Alexis laid this out plainly: if you learn to sell, you will never go broke. That is not hype. That is economics. Every business on earth needs someone who can communicate value and close a transaction. The person who can do that will always have options.

For parents who are juggling household responsibilities while trying to build something on the side, this matters enormously. You do not have four years to go back to school. You do not have a spare $50,000 lying around for a business investment. But if you can learn to have a confident conversation — to clearly explain why your product or service makes someone's life better — you have a foundation that no one can take from you.

---

## Four Weeks to Your First Client

Alexis has a system. And it is not complicated.

Her students typically go from zero to closing their first client in about four weeks. The reason that timeline works is not because the material is easy — it is because she forces action at every stage.

Consuming information feels productive. Watching another training, reading another book, saving another post — these activities feel like progress. But the breakthrough never comes from consuming. It comes from doing.

She builds action-oriented tasks into her curriculum from day one. Her students are in the field applying what they have learned before they feel ready. That discomfort is where the skill actually forms.

This is a principle that transfers well beyond sales. Whatever you are building — a business, a brand, a side income — the gap between learning and earning closes only when you start doing the thing before you feel fully prepared.

---

## Why Video Beats Every Other Cold Outreach Method

Here is a practical tip that Alexis shares with her students that separates the people who get responses from the ones who get ignored.

Stop sending cold text messages. Send video instead.

A short video — even thirty to sixty seconds recorded on your phone — allows a potential client to see your face, hear your voice, and sense your personality before they ever agree to a call. It breaks through the noise in a way that a text message simply cannot.

Think about your own inbox. How many cold texts or emails do you delete without reading? Now think about the last time someone sent you a personal video. Did you watch it? Almost certainly yes.

The goal is not to be flashy. The goal is to be human. When a prospect can see that you are a real person who genuinely believes in what you offer, the barrier to a conversation drops significantly.

For parent entrepreneurs who may feel like they do not have a "professional" setup — you do not need a studio. You need a phone, decent lighting, and something real to say.

## How to Handle "I Need to Think About It"

Every person in business will eventually hear some version of "I need to think about it." Most people handle this moment wrong — they either push too hard and lose the relationship, or they back off completely and lose the sale.

Alexis uses a three-step framework she calls Validate, Elevate, and Navigate.

*Validate* — Acknowledge what the prospect is feeling. "That makes complete sense. This is an important decision and I respect that you are taking it seriously."

*Elevate* — Honor their thoughtfulness. "The fact that you are not just jumping into things tells me you are intentional about how you invest. That is actually what I look for in a client."

*Navigate* — Gently move the conversation toward a solution. "Can I ask what would need to be true for this to feel like the right move for you right now?"

Notice what is not in this framework: pressure. There is no urgency tactic, no artificial deadline, no manipulation. This works because people do not resist being understood — they only resist being sold to. When a prospect feels genuinely heard, the conversation opens back up naturally.

## The Valley of Despair Is Not the End

Perhaps the most important thing Alexis shared in the entire conversation is something most people never warn you about before you start something new.

She calls it the valley of despair.

It works like this. You start a new business model or pursue a new income stream. Things feel exciting and full of possibility. Then, inevitably, it gets hard. Results slow down. Doubt creeps in. The excitement fades. And at that exact moment — the moment you are about to break through — most people quit.

They go find the next shiny opportunity and start over. Then they hit the same valley again. Then they quit again. And the cycle repeats until they conclude that nothing works, that the opportunity was a scam, that success is for other people.

Alexis's message is simple: the valley is not a sign that you chose wrong. It is a sign that you are exactly where growth happens. The people who come out on the other side of that valley are the ones who figured out what was not working, made adjustments, and stayed in the game.

Her advice for beginners comes down to three commitments:

1. Invest in your own skill set before you worry about the market. Stocks, crypto, and real estate all require capital. Your mouthpiece requires practice.

2. Find your community. You cannot reach your potential in isolation. Being around people who are winning changes what you believe is possible for yourself.

3. Remember that winning is the cure for burnout. As Alexis put it: "You are never tired when you are winning." The exhaustion most people feel is not from working too hard — it is from working hard without results. The answer is not to rest more. It is to get better until the wins start coming.

## The Bottom Line

If you are a parent who is also trying to build something — whether that is a sales career, a small business, a service, or a brand — the foundation Alexis describes is available to you right now. No degree. No credit score. No permission from anyone.

Your ability to communicate, connect, and clearly express the value of what you offer is worth more than most people ever realize. The question is whether you are willing to develop it.

For Alexis, investing in that skill changed everything. It can do the same for you.
