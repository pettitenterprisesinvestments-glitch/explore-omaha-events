Imagine walking through a lush, blooming garden when a towering dinosaur turns its head, lets out a thunderous roar, and sways its great tail just feet away. This summer, that prehistoric adventure is real at Lauritzen Gardens, where the traveling exhibition Dinosaurs Around the World: Passport to Pangea has taken up residence among the flowers and trees. For families looking for a memorable summer outing in Omaha, this one is hard to top.

The exhibition brings thirteen life-sized animatronic dinosaurs to the garden's stunning 100 acres. These are not static statues. They move, they roar, and they bring the ancient world to life in a way that delights children and surprises grown-ups too. As you wander the garden paths, you encounter the giant creatures staged among the greenery, each one a chance to learn about the age when dinosaurs ruled the earth. It is part nature walk, part time machine, and entirely fun.

Here is what your family needs to know before you go. Dinosaurs Around the World is on display at Lauritzen Gardens, located at 100 Bancroft Street in Omaha. The exhibition runs from May 30 through September 7, 2026, so there is plenty of summer left to catch it. The gardens are generally open from 9:00 a.m. to 4:30 p.m., and admission runs roughly fourteen to eighteen dollars depending on age, with the dinosaur exhibition included with garden admission. It is always a good idea to confirm current hours and pricing before your visit.

The beauty of this event is that it pairs two wonderful things in one trip. Lauritzen Gardens is a treasure in its own right, a sprawling botanical paradise with rose gardens, a model railroad garden, woodland trails, and seasonal blooms that make every visit beautiful. Adding the dinosaurs turns an already lovely outing into a genuine adventure. Kids who come for the dinosaurs leave having also wandered through one of the most peaceful and gorgeous spots in the city.

A few tips will make your visit smoother. Come earlier in the day during summer to enjoy cooler temperatures as you walk the grounds. Bring water, sunscreen, and comfortable shoes, since you will be strolling outdoors among the gardens. Let the little ones take their time at each dinosaur, where they can watch the movements and read about each species. And leave time to explore the rest of the gardens, which are well worth the visit on their own.

Outings like this are exactly what summer is made for, the kind of day that sparks a child's imagination and gives the whole family something to talk about on the ride home. Whether your kids are lifelong dinosaur fans or simply love a good adventure, a walk among these gentle giants is a summer memory in the making.

Don't wait too long, since the exhibition closes September 7.
