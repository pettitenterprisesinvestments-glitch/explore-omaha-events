If your family has a soft spot for that classic end-of-summer feeling, the kind that smells like kettle corn and sounds like a Ferris wheel turning against an evening sky, you have probably felt the absence of Omaha's Septemberfest. For decades it was the place to spend Labor Day weekend, but that beloved downtown tradition is no longer being held, and many local families have been hunting for a worthy way to fill its shoes. The good news is that one of the most cherished celebrations in the entire state is alive, well, and just a comfortable drive west: the Nebraska State Fair.

The Nebraska State Fair is an eleven-day celebration of everything that makes this state special, from agriculture and craftsmanship to music, food, and good old-fashioned carnival fun. It has been a gathering place for Nebraska families for generations, and in 2026 it lands right where Omaha families want it to be, ending on Labor Day itself. That makes it a natural fit for a weekend road trip or even a single big day out with the kids before the school routine fully takes hold.

Here is when and where to find it. The 2026 Nebraska State Fair runs from Friday, August 28 through Labor Day, Monday, September 7, 2026, at Fonner Park in Grand Island. The fairgrounds are located at 501 E. Fonner Park Road, Grand Island, NE 68801, roughly a hundred and fifty miles west of Omaha along Interstate 80, which makes it an easy and scenic drive for a day trip or a short family getaway. Because the fair spans two weekends and all the days in between, you have plenty of flexibility to pick a date that works for your crew, including that final Labor Day Monday when the celebration reaches its happy crescendo.

The family experience here is wonderfully full. Children can wander the livestock barns to meet cattle, sheep, goats, pigs, and horses up close, and the birthing pavilion is always a highlight where little ones can witness the wonder of new farm animals coming into the world. The midway carnival delivers exactly the rides and games you remember from your own childhood, from gentle spinners for the smallest visitors to taller thrills for the brave. Throughout the grounds you will find free entertainment, stage shows, competitive exhibits showcasing Nebraska-made goods and homegrown talent, and of course the legendary fair food, where shared funnel cakes and fresh lemonade somehow taste better than they have any right to.

What makes the State Fair such a great choice for families is the same thing that made Septemberfest feel like home. It is wholesome, it is welcoming, and it gathers neighbors from across the region in a spirit of community and gratitude. There is room here for every age and every budget, with plenty to see and do that costs nothing beyond gate admission, and an easy, unhurried pace that lets you linger over the things your kids love most. It is the kind of place where grandparents, parents, and children can all find their own favorite corner and still come together for a shared meal at a picnic table.

A few practical tips will help your day go smoothly. Late summer in central Nebraska can be warm, so pack water bottles, sunscreen, and hats, and plan a midday break in one of the shaded indoor exhibit halls when little legs get tired. Comfortable shoes are a must, as you will cover plenty of ground. Strollers are a smart call for younger children, and bringing a small backpack with snacks can save you a few stops. Admission is gated and ticketed, with carnival rides priced separately, so it is worth reviewing the latest gate and carnival pricing on the official website before you go, where you can also buy tickets in advance and check the daily schedule of concerts and free entertainment. If you are turning the trip into a longer weekend, the fair's website lists camping and lodging options near the grounds.

While you are mapping out the rest of your late-summer fun closer to home, you might enjoy our roundup of <a href="/article/25-free-things-omaha-summer">25 free things to do in Omaha this summer</a> for more budget-friendly family ideas. But for that big, joyful, Labor Day tradition the whole family will remember, point the car toward Grand Island. To confirm dates, hours, ticket prices, and the full lineup of entertainment, visit the official Nebraska State Fair website at <a href="https://www.statefair.org/">statefair.org</a> and start planning a day your family will be talking about long after the last ride lights go dim.
