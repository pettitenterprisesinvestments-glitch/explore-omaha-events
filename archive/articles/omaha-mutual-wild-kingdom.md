For a whole generation of American families, Sunday evening had a familiar rhythm. Dinner finished, the dishes cleared, everyone gathered in the living room, and the television flickered to life with one of the most beloved programs of its era. It opened with wildlife and adventure, and with a name that became part of the national vocabulary: Mutual of Omaha's Wild Kingdom. What many viewers across the country never fully realized was that this window into the wonders of the natural world was brought to them by a company rooted right here in Omaha, Nebraska.

Mutual of Omaha itself was already a proud part of the city long before it became a household name on television. Founded in Omaha back in 1909, the insurance company grew into one of the city's signature institutions, an anchor employer and a point of local pride for more than a century. But it was a bold leap into television that would make the Mutual of Omaha name famous in living rooms from coast to coast.

In 1963, the company began sponsoring a wildlife program called Wild Kingdom, and it became a phenomenon. Hosted by the genial zoologist Marlin Perkins, alongside his energetic and fearless co-host Jim Fowler, the show took American families on adventures to see lions, elephants, crocodiles, and creatures they had only ever read about. Week after week, it carried viewers from their own couches to the savannas, jungles, and rivers of the world. For millions of children, it was their very first encounter with the great diversity of life on Earth, and for many of them, it sparked a lifelong love of animals and nature.

Wild Kingdom was more than entertainment. In an age before endless nature documentaries and streaming wildlife footage, it played a real role in awakening Americans to the wonders of the natural world and the importance of caring for it. It made conservation feel exciting and personal. And it did all of this while carrying the name of an Omaha company proudly out into the world, so that for decades, people across America associated Omaha with adventure, wildlife, and Sunday nights with the family.

The original series ran for years and became woven into the fabric of American pop culture, so much so that the phrase Mutual of Omaha's Wild Kingdom still brings a warm wave of nostalgia to anyone who grew up with it. The beloved brand has been revived for new audiences over the years, carrying its spirit of wonder to a new generation. And Mutual of Omaha remains a cornerstone of the city, its name and its iconic presence still very much part of Omaha today.

There is something wonderful about the fact that one of the great shared memories of twentieth-century American family life came, in part, from Omaha. It is a reminder that this unassuming Heartland city has quietly shaped the wider culture in ways many people never realize, from the food on the nation's tables to the shows that gathered its families together.

So when you visit Omaha, take a little pride in walking the streets of the city that sent the wild kingdom into America's living rooms.
