There is something quietly wonderful about a weekend morning spent wandering rows of canopy tents, basket in hand, soaking up the smell of fresh bread and the colors of just-picked flowers. For generations of Omaha families, that morning has a name: the Omaha Farmers Market. Each year from late spring through the heart of fall, this beloved outdoor tradition fills two of the city's favorite gathering spots with farm-fresh produce, homemade treats, local makers, and the easy, friendly energy that only a neighborhood market can offer. Whether you are a longtime regular or planning your very first visit, the 2026 season is a wonderful excuse to slow down and enjoy a morning together.

Here is the simplest way to think about it: there are two markets, on two days, in two parts of town. The Saturday market sets up downtown in the historic Old Market at 11th and Jackson Streets, running from 8:00am to 12:30pm. The Sunday market gathers in Aksarben Village at 67th and Mercy Road, open a little later from 9:00am to 1:00pm. For 2026, the Saturday Old Market runs May 2 through October 10, and the Sunday Aksarben Village market runs May 3 through October 11. Both markets happen every single weekend in between, rain or shine, which means you can build a standing family tradition all summer and right on through the crisp, apple-scented weeks of fall. With the season carrying into September and October, there is still plenty of time to make a morning of it.

The real magic, of course, is in the strolling. You will find tables stacked with sweet corn, ripe tomatoes, leafy greens, berries, peppers, and whatever else is in season that week, much of it picked just a day or two before. There are bakers with warm bread and flaky pastries, vendors selling local honey, jam, cheese, roasted nuts, kettle corn, and mini donuts, and buckets overflowing with fresh-cut sunflowers and zinnias that make the perfect centerpiece for Sunday dinner. Hungry little ones can nibble on breakfast treats while you sip coffee, and you will often catch the gentle sound of live music drifting between the tents. Many weekends you will spot makers offering handmade soaps, candles, pottery, and crafts, so a quick walk can easily turn into finding a small treasure to take home.

It is an especially lovely outing for families because it asks so little and gives so much. There is no admission fee to browse and wander, the pace is unhurried, and there is room for children to walk, look, and learn where their food actually comes from. Striking up a conversation with the farmer who grew your tomatoes, letting a child pick out the prettiest bunch of flowers, or simply sharing a warm pastry on a bench together makes for the kind of simple, screen-free morning that sticks in a child's memory. Both markets welcome leashed pets too, so the family dog is invited to come along for the walk.

A few friendly tips will make your visit even smoother. Try to arrive early, especially if there is a particular vendor or produce you have your heart set on, because the freshest items and the best parking go quickly. Bring along a reusable tote or two and a little cash; while many vendors now take cards, cash keeps things simple, and both market locations have an on-site ATM if you need it. Parking is easy at both spots: in Aksarben Village the nearby garages are free, and downtown the Park Omaha Jackson Garage offers a free first hour. Strollers and wagons roll along just fine, so pack water, sunscreen, and a hat for the kids, and give yourselves time to linger rather than rush.

Part of what makes these markets feel so special is the way they fit into a bigger, well-loved part of the city. A Saturday trip pairs beautifully with a wander through the cobblestone charm of the <a href="/article/omaha-old-market">Old Market district</a>, and if you are gathering ideas for a budget-friendly summer, the market belongs right alongside our roundup of <a href="/article/25-free-things-omaha-summer">free things to do in Omaha this summer</a>. It is the kind of outing that feels generous to your wallet and your weekend alike.

So set the alarm a touch early, gather everyone up, and make your way to one of the markets this season. For the most up-to-date details on dates, hours, special events, and the latest vendor lineup, visit the official website at <a href="https://omahafarmersmarket.com/">omahafarmersmarket.com</a>.
