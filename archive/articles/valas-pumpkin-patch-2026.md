When the air turns crisp and the leaves begin to change around Omaha, one family tradition rises above all the rest. Vala's Pumpkin Patch in nearby Gretna is one of the largest fall festivals in the entire country, and its 2026 season opens on Saturday, September 5. If your family makes only one autumn outing this year, this is the one to circle on the calendar.

Vala's is not your average roadside pumpkin stand. Spread across 160 acres, it is a sprawling celebration of everything wonderful about fall. There are hundreds of pumpkin varieties to pick from, an apple orchard, fresh cider making, towering corn mazes, hayrides out to the fields, live entertainment, and dozens upon dozens of family activities tucked around every corner. You could visit several times during the season and still not see it all.

Here are the essentials for planning your trip. Vala's Pumpkin Patch is located at 12102 South 180th Street in Gretna, a short and easy drive from the Omaha metro. The 2026 season opens on September 5, with daily hours generally running from 9:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., and the festival continues through the fall. Admission tickets are required, and because Vala's is so popular, it is wise to plan your visit and check current dates, hours, and ticket details on the official website before you go. You can find all the planning information at <a href="https://valaspumpkinpatch.com/plan-your-visit/dates-hours/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Vala's official visitor page</a>.

What makes Vala's such a beloved tradition is that there is genuinely something for every age. Little ones love the friendly farm animals, the play areas, and the wagon rides. Older kids race through the corn mazes and line up for the slides and attractions. Parents and grandparents savor the cider, the kettle corn, the bonfires, and the simple pleasure of a beautiful fall day spent together outdoors. It is the kind of place where families build the memories they talk about for years.

A few tips will help you make the most of the day. Go early in the season or on a weekday if you can, since weekends in October draw big crowds. Wear comfortable shoes and dress for the weather, because you will be on your feet and outdoors for hours. Bring cash for some of the food stands, and plan to stay long enough to enjoy the patch after dark, when the bonfires are lit and the whole place takes on a magical glow. And of course, save room and time to pick the perfect pumpkin to take home.

Vala's is more than a festival. For countless Omaha-area families, it is the official start of fall, a yearly ritual as dependable as the changing leaves. Whether you are a longtime regular or visiting for the very first time, the 2026 season promises the same wholesome, old-fashioned fun that has made Vala's a regional treasure.

Mark September 5 on your calendar, gather the family, and get ready for a day you will all remember.
