There is a particular kind of magic that arrives in the Omaha area each summer, and it smells like kettle corn and fresh-cut hay. It is the sound of a 4-H youngster gently leading a calf into the show ring, the squeal of children spinning on a carnival ride against a wide Nebraska sky, and the easy laughter of neighbors catching up over a shared funnel cake. That magic has a name, and in 2026 it returns once again to Springfield: the 89th Annual <a href="https://sarpyfair.com/">Sarpy County Fair</a>.

The Sarpy County Fair is a true, old-fashioned county fair, the kind that has been knit into the fabric of this community for generations. Hosted by the Sarpy County Agricultural Society, it celebrates the farms, families, and faith-rooted small-town spirit that still shape life just beyond Omaha's edge. For 2026 the fair is set for July 29 through August 2, with the carnival and a crowd-favorite demolition derby rounding things out into Sunday, August 3. Because schedules can shift slightly from year to year, it is always wise to glance at the official site for the most current day-by-day lineup before you load up the car.

You will find it all at the Sarpy County Fairgrounds, located at 100 Main Street in Springfield, Nebraska, an easy and scenic drive south of the Omaha metro. Springfield is a welcoming little town, and pulling into the fairgrounds feels a bit like stepping back into a gentler, friendlier time. The grounds themselves are free to wander, with plenty of free entertainment scattered across the week, while signature events such as the carnival, tractor and truck pulls, extreme bull riding, the rodeo, and the live concerts are ticketed separately. That mix means a family can enjoy a wonderful afternoon strolling the barns and exhibits without spending a dime, then choose the special evening events that suit them best.

For many families, the heart of the fair lives in the livestock barns and the 4-H and Open Class exhibits. Here you will meet the young people of Sarpy County who have spent months raising animals, baking, sewing, growing vegetables, and building projects, all in the hope of earning a coveted blue ribbon. Walking these aisles with your children is a quiet gift. They get to pet a soft-eyed sheep, watch a proud young exhibitor groom a steer, and understand, perhaps for the first time, where their food really comes from and how much honest work goes into it. If your family enjoys this glimpse into the region's agricultural roots, you might also love exploring the history of the <a href="/article/omaha-stockyards">Omaha Stockyards</a>, another cornerstone of this area's heritage.

Of course, no county fair would be complete without the carnival. As dusk settles, the midway comes alive with spinning lights, cheerful music, and the happy chaos of rides for every age. Little ones find gentle merry-go-rounds and pint-sized adventures, while the bigger kids race toward the rides that flip the stomach and thrill the spirit. Unlimited-ride wristbands keep things simple for parents, so the children can ride to their hearts' content. Then there is the food, glorious fair food: corn dogs, fresh lemonade, roasted sweet corn, cotton candy, and treats fried to golden perfection that taste somehow better in the open summer air.

Evenings at the fair carry their own excitement, with the dust-and-grit drama of tractor pulls, the cowboy courage of the rodeo and extreme bull riding, toe-tapping live music, and the roaring, good-natured mayhem of the demolition derby. Add in the grand parade, free demonstrations, and the simple joy of running into half the people you know, and you have the makings of memories your children will carry for a lifetime.

What makes the Sarpy County Fair so wonderful for families is exactly this blend of wholesome fun and genuine community. It is affordable, it is unhurried, and it honors values worth passing down. A few practical tips will help your day go smoothly. Arrive early, especially on the weekend, to find easy parking and beat the heat. August in Nebraska can be hot and sunny, so pack hats, sunscreen, and refillable water bottles. Bring cash, as carnival rides, food vendors, and game booths often move faster with it, and strollers are a smart idea for tired little legs as the afternoon stretches on.

For more wallet-friendly inspiration, take a look at our roundup of <a href="/article/25-free-things-omaha-summer">25 free things to do in Omaha this summer</a>, then circle the Sarpy County Fair on your calendar. Whether you come for the livestock, the lights, the music, or simply the chance to gather with neighbors under a wide summer sky, this beloved tradition has a warm welcome waiting for your whole family.
