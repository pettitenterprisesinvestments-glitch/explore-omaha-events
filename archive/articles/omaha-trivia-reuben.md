Few sandwiches are as beloved as the Reuben: warm, toasted rye bread piled high with corned beef, melted Swiss cheese, tangy sauerkraut, and a swipe of dressing, grilled to gooey perfection. It is a deli classic found on menus all across America. But here is a piece of trivia that may surprise you the next time you order one: many people believe the Reuben sandwich was invented right here in Omaha, Nebraska, and the story behind it is as fun as the sandwich is delicious.

As Omaha lore tells it, the year was 1925, and the place was the elegant Blackstone Hotel. A group of local businessmen met there regularly for a late-night poker game, and one of the regulars was a grocer named Reuben Kulakofsky. During one of these games, as the story goes, the hungry players needed a hearty snack to keep them going, and Reuben dreamed up a stacked sandwich of corned beef, Swiss cheese, sauerkraut, and dressing on rye bread. It was a hit at the table.

It was such a hit, in fact, that the hotel's owner, Charles Schimmel, who was part of the poker group, loved it so much that he added it to the menu of the hotel's restaurant. He named it the Reuben, after the man who created it, and the sandwich quickly developed a devoted following. From that single Omaha hotel, the Reuben spread to delis and diners across the nation, eventually becoming one of the most famous sandwiches in America. By the 1950s it had won a national sandwich contest, cementing its place as a true American classic.

Now, it is worth a friendly note that a few other cities, including New York, have floated their own claims to the Reuben over the years, as often happens with beloved foods. But Omahans will tell you, with a grin and a great deal of pride, that their city is the true home of the Reuben, born at the Blackstone Hotel during a poker game nearly a century ago. It is a claim the city wears proudly, and the historic Blackstone neighborhood remains a fun, foodie-friendly part of town to this day.

The Blackstone Hotel, by the way, has another delicious claim to fame. That same Omaha hotel is also credited with introducing Butter Brickle ice cream, the rich vanilla-and-toffee treat that became a nostalgic favorite. So this one elegant Omaha address may have given the world both a legendary sandwich and a classic ice cream flavor, which is not a bad legacy for a single hotel.

This is exactly the kind of tasty trivia that makes Omaha such a fun city to visit. It is a place that has quietly contributed more to the American table than most people realize, from world-famous steaks to the TV dinner to the Reuben itself. Food lovers will find plenty to savor here, along with the stories that go with it.

So the next time you bite into a Reuben, you can share the fun fact that it may well have been born in Omaha. Better yet, come taste the city's food scene yourself and visit the historic Blackstone district where it all began.
