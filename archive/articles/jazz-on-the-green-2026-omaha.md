There is something almost magical about a summer evening in Omaha when the workday is done, the sun begins to slip behind the rooftops, and the sound of live music drifts across a wide green lawn. For more than four decades, that exact feeling has had a name in our city, and it is one that families look forward to all year long. Jazz on the Green is back for 2026, and it remains one of the most joyful, welcoming, and wonderfully free things you can do with the people you love all summer.

Presented by <a href="https://o-pa.org/learn-engage/jazz-on-the-green/">Omaha Performing Arts</a>, Jazz on the Green is a cherished outdoor concert series that brings local and touring musicians to one of the prettiest gathering spots in the city. The tradition actually began way back in 1985 on the lawn of the Joslyn Art Museum, when crowds grew from a few hundred curious listeners into thousands of neighbors packing picnics and sharing a beautiful night together. Today the series has found a perfect home at Turner Park, and the spirit behind it has never changed: it is a beautiful summer night in Omaha, and all are welcome.

Here is when and where to find it. Jazz on the Green takes place every Thursday from July 9 through August 13, 2026, six evenings in a row, at Turner Park at Midtown Crossing, located at 3110 Farnam Street in Omaha. The lawn opens at 5:00 PM so you have plenty of time to settle in, preshow entertainment begins at 6:30 PM, and the main performance starts at 7:30 PM. Best of all, the event is completely free and open to the public, with no ticket or registration required. You simply show up, find a patch of grass, and enjoy.

The 2026 lineup offers a wonderful variety of sounds. The season opens on July 9 with the USAF Heartland of America Band "Raptor," followed by CJ Chenier and The Red Hot Louisiana Band on July 16, José Valentino and the UNO Latin Jazz Ensemble with Michael Pujado on July 23, J and The Causeways on July 30, Brittany Davis and Black Thunder on August 6, and Braxton Cook closing out the series on August 13. From high-energy zydeco to smooth, soulful jazz, there is a flavor of music for nearly every taste, and that mix is part of what makes each Thursday feel a little different and a little special.

For families, Jazz on the Green is a true gift. The wide open lawn gives little ones room to wiggle, dance, and clap along without anyone minding, and the relaxed, come-as-you-are atmosphere means you never have to worry about keeping children perfectly still in a quiet theater. Older kids and teens often enjoy discovering live instruments up close, and grandparents love the easygoing pace and the chance to share a tradition many of them remember from years past. It is the kind of evening where three generations can sit on the same blanket and all walk away smiling.

A few practical tips will help your night go smoothly. Plan to arrive early, ideally close to when the lawn opens at 5:00 PM, because the best spots fill up as showtime approaches and the early hour gives you a calm, unhurried start. Bring a blanket or some folding lawn chairs, and do not forget sunscreen for the early evening sun and bug spray as dusk settles in. You are welcome to pack a picnic, whether that is a simple basket of sandwiches and fruit or a full spread to share, and the restaurants right there at Midtown Crossing offer plenty of dine-in and take-out options if you would rather not cook. Leashed, well-behaved pets are welcome too, so the family dog can join the fun. For parking, there are nearby garages and street parking around Midtown Crossing, so it helps to map your route ahead of time and leave a little buffer for the walk to the lawn.

If you are looking to fill your calendar with more no-cost summer fun, Jazz on the Green pairs perfectly with the other gems our city offers, and you can find plenty of ideas in our roundup of <a href="/article/25-free-things-omaha-summer">25 free things to do in Omaha this summer</a>. Make a whole evening of it, stay for the music as the lights come up on the stage, and let the kids remember the night the whole family sat under the open sky together.

So mark those Thursdays on the calendar, gather your blanket and your favorite people, and come be part of a tradition that has been bringing Omaha together for forty years and counting. For the most up-to-date details, weather announcements, and the full schedule, visit the <a href="https://o-pa.org/learn-engage/jazz-on-the-green/">official Jazz on the Green page</a>. We will see you on the lawn.
