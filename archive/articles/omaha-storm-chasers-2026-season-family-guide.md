There is something timeless about a summer evening at the ballpark. The smell of fresh popcorn drifting on the breeze, the satisfying crack of a bat, kids in oversized caps cheering for a player they just met on the field, and a sky full of fireworks to send everyone home smiling. For families across the Omaha metro, that experience has a name and a home: the Omaha Storm Chasers at Werner Park. The 2026 season is officially underway, and it is shaping up to be another wonderful summer of affordable, wholesome fun that the whole family can enjoy together.

The Storm Chasers are the Triple-A affiliate of the Kansas City Royals, which means the baseball you will watch is top-notch. These are talented athletes often just one phone call away from the big leagues, so you may very well be cheering for a future Major League star. But here is the secret that local families have known for years: you do not have to be a die-hard baseball fan to fall in love with a night at Werner Park. The atmosphere is relaxed, joyful, and built from the ground up with families in mind.

You will find the ballpark at 12356 Ballpark Way in Papillion, just southwest of Omaha and easy to reach from anywhere in the metro. Werner Park is a gem of a venue, intimate enough that there is not a bad seat in the house, yet packed with thoughtful touches. One of its best features is the enormous grass berm that wraps around much of the outfield, one of the largest in all of Minor League Baseball. Berm seating is general admission and first-come, first-served, so you can spread out a blanket, let the little ones wiggle and roam, and enjoy the game at your own pace. Best of all, those berm tickets are among the most budget-friendly in town, making it remarkably easy to treat the whole family without straining the wallet.

The family-friendly traditions are where Werner Park truly shines. The Storm Chasers run a generous lineup of promotions all season long. Friday nights mean Fireworks Friday, with a dazzling fireworks show lighting up the sky after the game on Fridays from May through early September, a guaranteed hit with kids and grown-ups alike. Sundays bring Sunday Funday, a celebration designed for families that has featured face painters, balloon artists, airbrush tattoos, and visits from the team mascots. And no Sunday is complete without Kids Run the Bases, where children twelve and under get to charge around the basepaths after the final out, living out a big-league dream of their own. Dog-loving families can even look for Bark in the Park nights, when well-behaved pups are welcome to join the fun in the berm. Because the exact giveaway dates, theme nights, and special events shift from week to week, the smartest move is to check the official schedule before you go so you can plan your visit around the promotion your family loves most.

Why is this such a great outing for families? It comes down to value, variety, and ease. For the price of a single movie ticket, you can give your kids hours of fresh air, live entertainment, and memories that last far longer than any screen. There is room to move, plenty of kid-friendly food, friendly mascots to high-five, and the kind of easygoing pace where nobody minds if a toddler needs a stretch between innings. It is the rare night out where every age, from grandparents to newborns, finds something to enjoy.

A few practical tips will help your evening go smoothly. Parking in the main Werner Park lot is free and just steps from the gates, so arrive a little early to settle in, grab snacks, and let the kids explore before first pitch. Since many games happen under the warm summer sun and stay outdoors into the evening, bring sunscreen, a hat, and a light layer for when the temperature dips after sundown. If you are claiming a spot on the berm, blankets and towels are welcome, though lawn chairs are not, so pack accordingly. Tickets are easy to grab online ahead of time, which is especially wise for popular fireworks and weekend dates that tend to fill up.

When you are ready to pick your night, head over to the official Storm Chasers site at <a href="https://www.milb.com/omaha">milb.com/omaha</a> to browse the full 2026 schedule, check promotions, and grab your tickets. We will see you on the berm, and do not forget to stay for the fireworks.
