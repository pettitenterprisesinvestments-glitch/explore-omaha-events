Tucked into a leafy historic neighborhood in midtown Omaha stands something you might not expect to find in the heart of the Great Plains: a genuine castle. With its grand stone towers, turrets, and carved details, Joslyn Castle looks like it stepped straight out of a storybook, and the tale of the remarkable couple who built it is every bit as memorable as the mansion itself.

The castle was built in 1903 by George and Sarah Joslyn, one of the wealthiest and most generous couples in Omaha's history. George Joslyn had made a great fortune in the newspaper and printing business, and the couple chose to build a home worthy of a fairy tale. Designed in the elegant Scottish Baronial style and officially named Lynhurst, the mansion featured dozens of rooms filled with beautiful craftsmanship, set on grounds landscaped with gardens, greenhouses, and stately trees. It quickly became the centerpiece of the fashionable neighborhood that came to be known as the Gold Coast, and it remains one of the most distinctive historic homes in the entire region.

But the Joslyns are remembered for far more than their grand house. They were extraordinarily devoted to Omaha and to giving back to their community. When George passed away, Sarah Joslyn carried their shared spirit of generosity forward in a way that would shape the cultural life of the city for generations. In her husband's memory, she funded the creation of the magnificent Joslyn Art Museum, a gift to the people of Omaha that remains one of the finest art museums in the country to this day. The same name that graces the castle also graces one of the city's greatest cultural treasures, a lasting testament to one family's love for their city.

That connection is part of what makes Joslyn Castle so special to visit. It is not just a beautiful old mansion, but a window into the lives of people whose generosity still enriches Omaha more than a century later. Walking the grounds and admiring the stonework, you can imagine the gatherings once held here and feel the presence of a couple who believed that great wealth came with a responsibility to give back.

Today, Joslyn Castle is lovingly preserved and listed on the National Register of Historic Places. It serves the community as a historic site and a venue for tours, events, and cultural gatherings, keeping its grand halls alive with activity rather than letting them fade into silence. For visitors, it offers the rare chance to step inside a true turn-of-the-century castle and experience the elegance of a bygone era, right in the middle of a modern American city.

Joslyn Castle is a perfect example of the surprising delights that await travelers in Omaha. This is a city full of unexpected treasures, places that reward the curious visitor with beauty, history, and great stories. Pair a visit to the castle with a trip to the nearby Joslyn Art Museum, and you can experience both halves of the Joslyns' incredible legacy in a single, memorable day.

So when you plan your trip to Omaha, make room for a visit to Joslyn Castle. Admire its storybook towers, learn the moving story of George and Sarah, and discover one of the city's most enchanting landmarks.
